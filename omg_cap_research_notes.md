# OMG-CAP VDMK Presentation Research Notes

## VDMK (Varlığa Dayalı Menkul Kıymet) Overview

### Definition
- Asset-Backed Securities (ABS) in Turkish context
- Converts receivables (senet) or collateralized receivables into marketable securities
- Alternative to conventional bank credit for real sector financing
- Widely used internationally

### Benefits

**For Investors:**
- Collateralized, secure structure
- Diversified portfolio
- High-yield investment option

**For Source Institution (Kaynak Kuruluş):**
- Asset-liability management
- Liquidity for balance sheet
- Low-cost alternative funding source

### Eligible Assets for VDMK
- Consumer loans
- Financial leasing contracts receivables
- Commercial receivables from invoiced sales (backed by promissory notes or collateral)
- Receivables from corporations (non-financial institutions) engaged in goods/services production

### Key Structure Elements

**Varlık Finansmanı Fonu (VFF):**
- Asset Finance Fund
- Created through fund charter based on fiduciary ownership principles
- No legal personality
- Holds assets on behalf of VDMK holders

**Kaynak Kuruluş (Source Institution):**
- Legal entity transferring assets to Fund portfolio

**Kurucu (Founder):**
- Banks, financial leasing/financing institutions, full-service brokerage firms
- Authorized to establish VFF

**Hizmet Sağlayıcı (Servicer):**
- Responsible for daily management of fund portfolio assets
- Can be Founder or another qualified Source Institution
- Handles collection, follow-up, and transfer to Fund accounts

**Operasyon Sorumlusu (Operations Manager):**
- Handles Fund Board responsibilities
- Fund accounting system, document recording
- Preparation of issuance documents
- Investor information

**Fon Kurulu (Fund Board):**
- 3-person board appointed by Founder
- At least one independent member required
- At least one member must hold SPK Level 3 License

### Quality Enhancement Mechanisms
- Insurance, guarantee, letter of guarantee
- Additional collateral exceeding total fund liabilities (per banking standards)

### SPK Compliance
- Sold to qualified investors per SPK definition
- Subject to SPK regulations and approval
- Each VDMK has ISIN code (similar to bonds)

## Turkish VDMK Market Context

### Regulatory Framework
- Governed by SPK (Sermaye Piyasası Kurulu / Capital Markets Board)
- First ABS issuance in Turkey: October 20, 2011 (Borsa Istanbul)
- Requires SPK approval for each issuance
- Rating agencies: TurkRating, others provide national ratings

### Market Characteristics
- Growing alternative financing market
- Sectors: e-commerce, automotive, agriculture, chemicals, retail
- Qualified investor market
- Digital platforms available for subscription (e.g., PASHA Bank platform)

### Typical Structure
- 6-12 month average maturity
- Discount structure (e.g., 8% illustrative)
- Risk retention (e.g., 5% by anchor investor)
- Regular cash flow from collections
- Servicer manages daily operations

## TSPB (Türkiye Sermaye Piyasaları Birliği) / Turkish Capital Markets Association

### Organization Role
- Self-regulatory organization for Turkish capital markets
- Represents brokerage firms, portfolio management companies, and other capital market intermediaries
- Provides investor education and protection
- Issues regulations and guidelines for members
- Publishes market data and statistics

### Relation to VDMK Market
- Provides glossary and educational materials on VDMK
- Members include institutions authorized to issue and trade VDMK
- Supports market development and standardization
- Coordinates with SPK on regulatory matters

- User mentioned "TKMS" as partner
